The Science of Brown Noise

What is Brown Noise?

Brown noise, also known as Brownian noise or red noise, is a type of sound signal with a power density that decreases 6 dB per octave with increasing frequency. This gives it deeper, richer bass frequencies compared to white or pink noise, creating a rumbling, soothing sound reminiscent of a waterfall or heavy rainfall.

Why Brown Noise for Sleep?

The deeper frequencies in brown noise are particularly effective at masking environmental sounds that might disturb your sleep. The consistent, low-frequency rumble helps your brain tune out sudden noises while creating a calming audio environment that promotes deeper, more restful sleep.

The Deep Brown Noise Difference

SleepDBN uses advanced audio engineering to generate brown noise with enhanced low-frequency content. This "deep" brown noise provides even more effective sound masking and a more immersive relaxation experience than standard brown noise generators.
